Introduction
Why does the world need to change its energy habits?
🇵🇭
Philippine Context
The Philippines and many countries have depended on coal, oil, and natural gas for over a century. With rising fuel prices and environmental damage, the shift to alternative energy is now more urgent than ever.

For the past hundred years, the world has relied on the same fossil fuels — coal, gasoline, and natural gas. While these fuels have powered modern civilization, burning them releases enormous amounts of carbon dioxide (CO₂), driving global warming and climate change.

The rate of consumption is alarming. Scientists and governments around the world are now searching for energy resources that are cleaner, more economical, and environmentally sustainable — benefiting both present and future generations.

What is Alternative Energy? Alternative energy refers to any energy source that serves as a substitute for fossil fuels (coal, gasoline, natural gas). These alternatives address concerns like high carbon dioxide emissions and global warming. Alternative energy is a broader category encompassing all non-fossil-fuel energy sources — renewable energies are only a part of it.

What is Renewable Energy? Renewable energy comes from naturally occurring sources that replenish on their own — solar, wind, geothermal, hydro, biomass, waves, and tides. The most abundant is solar energy.

Feature 🌿 Renewable Energy ⚡ Alternative Energy
Naturally replenishes? ✓ Yes — always Not always
Includes solar, wind, hydro? ✓ Yes ✓ Yes
Includes natural gas / fuel cells? ✗ No ✓ Yes (lower emissions)
Reduces carbon emissions? ✓ Yes ✓ Yes
Infinite in supply? ✓ Yes Not necessarily

Lesson 1: Energy Crisis
Understanding the roots of the global energy problem

Energy Crisis refers to any significant decrease in the supply of energy resources to an economy at a certain time and place. It affects electric power supply, fuel for machinery, and transportation.

Industrial Revolution (1800s–1900s)
Rise of Fossil Fuel Dependency
Industrialization and population growth drove skyrocketing demand for coal, oil, and natural gas. The world became deeply dependent on these finite resources.
2000s Energy Crisis
Oil Hits Record Highs
Middle East tensions, the falling U.S. dollar, peak oil concerns, and price speculation triggered a major crisis. Oil hit an all-time high of $147.30 per barrel in 2008.
2018 — Philippines
TRAIN Law Impact
World oil prices tripled. Diesel in the Philippines reached ₱41.10/liter (June 2018). The TRAIN Law's excise tax adjustments further raised diesel and gasoline prices, highlighting the urgency of alternative energy development.
Present Day
Global Push for Alternative Energy
Countries worldwide are investing heavily in solar, wind, geothermal, and other renewable technologies. The Philippines, with its volcanic geography and tropical climate, has great potential for multiple energy sources.
💡
Why does the Energy Crisis exist?
The energy crisis arises from a combination of factors: rapid population growth, industrial expansion, geopolitical instability in oil-producing regions, finite fossil fuel reserves, and the lack of sufficient investment in alternative energy infrastructure. As global demand continues to rise, supply cannot keep up — making the transition to alternative energy not just beneficial, but necessary.

Lessons 2 & 3: Alternative Energy Sources
Explore each energy type — click a tab to learn more

💧 Hydroelectric Energy

The potential energy stored in water held in dams drives a water turbine and generator, producing hydroelectric power. This is currently the most widely adopted alternative energy source worldwide.

✅ Advantage 💰 Water source is free 🔄 Virtually continuous generation ♻️ Water can be reused 🌱 No chemical process — clean energy

☀️ Solar Energy

Solar energy is the energy Earth receives from the sun — one of the most promising alternative sources, available for centuries to come. Energy is generated by photovoltaic (PV) cells that convert sunlight directly into electricity. Solar energy is also used for water heating and cooking.

✅ Advantage 💡 Source (sunlight) is free 🌙 Stored for nighttime use 🏡 Works in rural/remote areas 🌱 Clean generation ♾️ Available for thousands of years

💨 Wind Energy

Wind energy harnesses the power of moving air to spin wind turbine blades connected to an electric generator. It is most effective in areas with high, consistent wind velocity.

✅ Advantage 🌬️ Wind source is free 🌱 Clean — zero emissions ♾️ Renewable 🌏 Suitable for coastal Philippines

🌾 Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is derived from waste materials of human and animal activities — byproducts of the timber industry, agricultural yields (rice husks, sugarcane bagasse), and municipal solid waste. It uniquely turns waste into useful energy.

✅ Advantage ♻️ Turns waste into energy 🌱 Biological mass recycled naturally 🔄 Renewable — part of natural cycle 🇵🇭 Huge potential in PH agriculture

🌋 Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy taps heat from the Earth's interior. Steam and hot water from underground reservoirs drive turbines to generate electricity. The Philippines is one of the world's top geothermal energy producers, thanks to its volcanic geography.

✅ Advantage 🆓 Source is free of cost 🌿 No harmful byproducts ⚡ Consistent 24/7 power 🇵🇭 Ideal for volcanic Philippines

🌊 Tidal & Wave Energy

Tidal energy uses the gravitational pull of the sun and moon to create predictable tidal rises and falls, channeled through turbines. Wave energy uses the force of ocean waves (not tides) and involves floating structures on the ocean surface — causing less harm to sea life. Both are clean and renewable.

✅ Advantage 🆓 Free & renewable source 🌱 Clean — no pollution 📈 Predictable (tidal) 🐟 Low impact on marine life (wave)

🌡️ Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion (OTEC)

OTEC uses the temperature difference between warm tropical surface water and cold deep ocean water to generate electricity. It operates 24/7, year-round, making it ideal for tropical island nations like the Philippines. It also produces desalinated water and supports mariculture (sea farming).

✅ Advantage ⏰ 24/7 baseload electricity 🌱 Clean energy source 💧 Produces fresh water 🐠 Supports mariculture 🇵🇭 Ideal for tropical PH islands

Challenges with Alternative Energy Sources

⚠️ Why isn't everyone using alternative energy yet?
▼
  • Research phase: Many sources (wave, OTEC) are still being developed and optimized for large-scale use.
  • Scale challenge: While small systems are feasible, large-scale power generation remains difficult — hydroelectric is a key exception.
  • Infrastructure cost: Transitioning requires decommissioning old power plants and building entirely new grids — an enormous upfront investment.
  • Intermittency: Solar works only with sunlight; wind requires adequate breeze. Storage technology (batteries) must improve.
  • Phased approach: Most governments plan a gradual shift rather than an immediate transition — it must be done in phases.
🌍
Why should we switch NOW?
Alternative energy sources are free, clean, and do not burden the environment. If we shift to these sources, CO₂ emissions will decrease significantly, global warming will slow, and our fast-depleting fossil fuel reserves can be preserved for future generations. The damage caused since the Industrial Revolution is enormous — but action now can still make a difference.

Lesson Summary
Key takeaways from Lesson 15
⚡
Energy Crisis
A significant decrease in energy supply caused by overconsumption of finite fossil fuels, geopolitical instability, and population growth.
🌿
Alternative Energy
Any energy source that replaces fossil fuels. Broader than renewable — includes fuel cells and natural gas cogeneration.
☀️
Solar Energy
PV cells convert sunlight to electricity. Free, clean, and will be available for thousands of years.
💧
Hydroelectric
Most widely adopted alternative energy. Uses stored water energy to drive turbines — clean and continuous.
💨
Wind Energy
Wind turbines convert kinetic energy of wind to electricity. Ideal for coastal and highland areas in the Philippines.
🌾
Biomass
Converts agricultural, timber, and municipal wastes into energy — recycling matter through the biological cycle.
🌋
Geothermal
Taps Earth's heat through volcanic activity. Philippines is a world leader in geothermal energy production.
🌡️
OTEC
Uses ocean temperature differences to generate 24/7 baseload power. Also produces fresh water and supports sea farming.
